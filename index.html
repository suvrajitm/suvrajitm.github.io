About

A short introduction

I’m a Senior Research Scientist in computational biology, working at the intersection of structural biophysics, systems biology, and genomics.

At NYU Langone’s Applied Bioinformatics Laboratories & Department of Pathology , I support the Translational Research Program — collaborating across labs whose questions span genomics data analysis, structural biophysics, simulation, and prediction.

Previously, as Associate Research Scientist at Columbia University Irving Medical Center, I worked on computational methods for RNA–protein binding interactions and complex-structure prediction from crosslinking, sequence, and structural data. Before that, I trained for nearly a decade with Dr. Joachim Frank — Nobel Laureate (2017) — developing methods to recover the structure and dynamics of macromolecular machines from cryo-EM images.
